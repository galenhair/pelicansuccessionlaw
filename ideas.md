# Pelican Succession Law — Design Brainstorm

## Response 1
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Dark Institutional Modernism — the visual language of elite private banks and white-shoe law firms, updated for the digital age.

**Core Principles:**
- Authority through restraint: every element earns its place
- Deep navy as the primary surface, not black — warmer, more trustworthy
- Gold as a precision accent, never decorative noise
- Typography does the heavy lifting; imagery is secondary

**Color Philosophy:** Deep navy (#0B1628) as the dominant background signals stability and depth — the color of a judge's robe, not a startup. Gold (#C9A84C) is used sparingly as a power accent on CTAs, dividers, and key data points. Warm white (#F5F0E8) for body text creates a parchment-like warmth that references legal documents without being literal.

**Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial grid. Hero section splits: left two-thirds carries the headline and CTA; right one-third carries a floating "proof card" (case count, avg close time, Google rating). Sections alternate between full-bleed navy and narrow white-on-navy text columns.

**Signature Elements:**
- A minimal pelican silhouette (single-line art) used as the logo mark
- A thin gold horizontal rule used as a section divider throughout
- A "stat ticker" bar below the hero showing live proof points

**Interaction Philosophy:** Measured, deliberate. Hover states use a slow gold underline sweep. Scroll animations are subtle fade-ups, never bouncy. The site should feel like it moves with confidence, not urgency.

**Animation:** Sections fade up on scroll with 300ms ease-out. CTA buttons have a gold border that draws in from left on hover. The hero headline uses a staggered word-by-word reveal on load.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif) — bold, authoritative, legal heritage
- Body: DM Sans — clean, modern, readable at small sizes
- Accent labels: DM Mono (monospace) — used for stat numbers and step labels
</text>

## Response 2
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Civic Brutalism — the visual confidence of a courthouse facade translated into a digital interface.

**Core Principles:**
- Strong geometric structure: columns, grids, hard edges
- Contrast as communication: near-black vs. bright gold
- No softness — rounded corners are minimal, shadows are sharp
- Credibility through density: information-rich without clutter

**Color Philosophy:** Near-black (#111111) backgrounds with gold (#D4A843) as the primary action color and stark white for text. A deep slate (#1E2A3A) used as a secondary surface for cards and sidebars.

**Layout Paradigm:** Column-based grid that references classical architecture. The nav uses a full-width bar with the firm name left-aligned and the CTA right-aligned — no centered logos. Sections use a 12-column grid with deliberate asymmetry.

**Signature Elements:**
- Thick gold left-border accent on blockquotes and callout boxes
- Step numbers rendered in large, faded gold behind the step content
- A horizontal "case counter" that animates upward on scroll

**Interaction Philosophy:** Immediate and direct. No hover delays. Buttons invert instantly on hover (gold bg → dark text). The site respects the user's time.

**Animation:** Minimal. Counter animations on scroll. Section headers slide in from left. No parallax.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Bebas Neue — commanding, all-caps headlines
- Body: Source Serif 4 — readable, professional, legal credibility
- Labels: Space Mono — technical precision for numbers and tags
</text>

## Response 3 — SELECTED
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Southern Gothic Modernism — the warmth and weight of Louisiana's legal heritage fused with clean, modern digital design.

**Core Principles:**
- Deep navy as the primary canvas — trustworthy, authoritative, distinctly Southern
- Gold as the accent of earned credibility, not decoration
- Generous whitespace within dark sections creates breathing room and premium feel
- The pelican as a quiet brand anchor — present but never loud

**Color Philosophy:** Primary surface is deep navy (#0D1B2A). Gold (#C9A050) is used for CTAs, key numbers, and the logo mark. Warm white (#F8F4EE) for primary text. A lighter navy (#162236) for card backgrounds. The palette reads as premium legal, not tech startup.

**Layout Paradigm:** Vertical editorial flow with a strong left-anchored hierarchy. The hero uses a full-bleed dark section with the headline anchored left and a floating proof-point card right. Below the fold, alternating sections use a wide left text column with a narrow right sidebar for supporting data. This avoids the generic centered-everything layout.

**Signature Elements:**
- Minimal pelican line-art logo mark (single-stroke, geometric)
- Gold thin-rule dividers between major sections
- Stat cards with large gold numbers and small white labels

**Interaction Philosophy:** Confident and measured. Hover states are deliberate — gold underlines sweep in, buttons fill with gold. Nothing bounces or pulses. The site moves like a senior partner walks into a courtroom.

**Animation:** Hero headline fades in word by word on load (400ms stagger). Section content fades up on scroll with 350ms ease-out. CTA button has a gold shimmer sweep on hover. Stat numbers count up when they enter the viewport.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Cormorant Garamond (serif, bold) — old-world legal authority with elegance
- Body: DM Sans (sans-serif) — clean, modern, highly readable
- Numbers/Labels: DM Mono — precision and credibility for stats and step numbers
</text>
