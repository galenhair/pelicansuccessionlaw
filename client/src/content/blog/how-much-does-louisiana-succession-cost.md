## The Two Categories of Succession Costs

Louisiana succession costs fall into two categories: **attorney fees** and **court costs**. Understanding both is essential to budgeting for the process.

## Attorney Fees

Attorney fees are the largest component of succession costs. There are two common fee structures:

### Hourly Billing (Traditional)

Many Louisiana succession attorneys charge by the hour, typically $250–$450 per hour. A straightforward succession might take 8–15 hours of attorney time, resulting in fees of $2,000–$6,750 or more. Complex successions can easily exceed $10,000 in attorney fees.

The problem with hourly billing is **unpredictability**. Families dealing with grief should not also be worrying about whether a phone call or email will add to their bill.

## Court Filing Fees

Court filing fees are set by the Louisiana legislature and vary by parish. They typically range from **$150 to $350** for a standard succession. We advance the filing fee on your behalf and bill it to you at cost — no markup.

## Other Potential Costs

- **Certified death certificates**: $7–$15 each from the Louisiana Department of Health. You may need 3–5 copies.
- **Property appraisals**: Required for some complex estates. Typically $300–$600 per property.
- **Notary fees**: For documents signed outside our office. Typically $25–$75.
- **Recording fees**: For recording the Judgment of Possession in parish conveyance records. Typically $50–$150 per parish.
