## What Is the Louisiana Small Succession Affidavit?

The Louisiana Small Succession Affidavit is a legal document that allows heirs to transfer a deceased person's assets **without opening a formal court succession**. It is authorized by Louisiana Revised Statute 9:1421 and is one of the most useful tools in Louisiana succession law for qualifying estates.

## When Can You Use the Small Succession Affidavit?

The affidavit is available when ALL of the following conditions are met:

1. **The total value of the estate is $125,000 or less** (as of 2024)
2. **The estate does not include immovable property** (real estate, mineral interests, or other property attached to the land)
3. **At least 30 days have passed** since the date of death
4. **No succession has been opened** in any Louisiana court

If the estate includes real estate — even a small camp or vacant lot — the Small Succession Affidavit cannot be used for that property.

## What Can the Affidavit Transfer?

The affidavit can be used to transfer:

- Bank accounts and savings accounts
- Vehicles (through the Louisiana OMV)
- Investment accounts and brokerage accounts
- Life insurance proceeds (in some cases)
- Personal property

It **cannot** transfer real estate or mineral rights. Those require a formal succession.

## What Documents Are Required?

To prepare a valid Small Succession Affidavit, you will need:

- A certified copy of the death certificate
- The names and addresses of all heirs
- A description of the assets to be transferred
- Proof of heirship (birth certificates, marriage certificate, etc.)
- The original will, if one exists

The affidavit must be signed before a notary public and two witnesses.

## Limitations of the Small Succession Affidavit

The affidavit is a powerful tool, but it has real limitations:

- **Financial institutions can refuse it.** Banks are not required to honor the affidavit, though most will if it is properly prepared.
- **It does not protect against creditor claims.** Heirs who use the affidavit remain personally liable for the decedent's debts up to the value of the assets received.
- **It cannot be used if there is a dispute.** If heirs disagree about the distribution, a formal succession is required.

While the Small Succession Affidavit does not require court involvement, it does require precise legal drafting. An improperly prepared affidavit will be rejected by banks and the OMV.
