## The Core Documents for Every Louisiana Succession

Regardless of the complexity of the estate, every Louisiana succession requires the same core set of documents. Gathering these before you contact an attorney will speed up the process significantly.

## 1. Certified Death Certificate

You need a **certified copy** of the death certificate — not the informational copy provided by the funeral home. Certified copies have a raised seal and are issued by the Louisiana Department of Health (if the deceased died in Louisiana) or the vital records office of the state where the deceased died.

**How many do you need?** Order at least 3–5 certified copies. You will need one for the court filing, one for each financial institution, and one for each parish where real estate is located.

**How to order**: Online at ldh.la.gov or in person at the Louisiana Vital Records Registry in New Orleans.

## 2. The Original Will (If One Exists)

If the deceased left a will, you need the **original signed document** — not a photocopy. The original will must be filed with the court.

If you cannot locate the original will, check:
- The deceased's personal papers and safe deposit box
- The office of the notary who prepared the will
- The Louisiana Notarial Archives in New Orleans (wills can be registered there)
- The clerk of court in the parish of the deceased's domicile

## 3. Property Documents

For each piece of real estate owned by the deceased, you need:

- The **deed** showing the deceased's ownership interest (available from the parish clerk of court's conveyance records)
- The **current tax assessment** (available from the parish assessor's office)
- The **mortgage payoff statement** if the property has an outstanding mortgage

## 4. Heir Identification Documents

For each heir, you need:

- **Full legal name** and current address
- **Date of birth**
- **Social Security number**
- **Relationship to the deceased**

For children heirs: **birth certificates** showing the parent-child relationship.
For a surviving spouse: **marriage certificate**.
For adopted children: **adoption decree**.

## 5. Financial Account Information

For each bank account, investment account, or retirement account:

- Account number and financial institution name
- Approximate current balance
- Whether the account has a named beneficiary

Note: Accounts with named beneficiaries (POD accounts, IRAs with beneficiary designations) typically pass outside the succession and do not need to be included.

## 6. Vehicle Titles

For each vehicle owned by the deceased:

- The **original certificate of title**
- The **current odometer reading**

## 7. Business Interests

If the deceased owned an interest in a business (LLC, corporation, partnership):

- The **operating agreement** or **partnership agreement**
- The **buy-sell agreement**, if one exists
- The **most recent tax return** for the business
