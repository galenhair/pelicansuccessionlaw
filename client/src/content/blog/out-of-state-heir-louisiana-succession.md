## You Don't Need to Come to Louisiana

One of the most common misconceptions about Louisiana successions is that heirs must appear in person at the courthouse or at an attorney's office in Louisiana. This is not true.

At Pelican Succession Law, we handle successions **entirely remotely** for out-of-state heirs. Everything is done by phone, email, and e-signature. The only in-person step is signing one notarized document — which you can do at any notary public near you, anywhere in the country.

## Why Louisiana Successions Are Different for Out-of-State Heirs

Louisiana's civil law system creates unique requirements that out-of-state heirs often encounter for the first time:

- **Ancillary succession**: If the deceased lived in another state but owned Louisiana real estate, a Louisiana succession must be opened in Louisiana regardless of where the estate is being administered.
- **Forced heirship**: Louisiana's forced heirship laws may affect the distribution of the estate even if the deceased had a valid will from another state.
- **Community property**: Louisiana's community property rules may apply to assets acquired during a marriage, even if the couple lived in a community property state and later moved.

## The Remote Succession Process: Step by Step

### Step 1: Free Consultation (Phone or Video)
We start with a 30-minute consultation to understand the estate, identify the assets, and confirm whether a formal succession or Small Succession Affidavit is appropriate.

### Step 2: Document Collection (Email and Mail)
We send you a checklist of required documents. Most can be emailed as PDFs. The certified death certificate must be mailed or ordered directly from the Louisiana Department of Health.

### Step 3: Preparation (Our Office)
We prepare the Petition for Possession, Descriptive List of Assets, and all supporting documents. We handle all communication with the parish court.

### Step 4: Signing (Your Location)
We send you a Verification document to sign in front of a notary public. Any notary in any state can notarize this document. We provide clear instructions.

### Step 5: Filing and Judgment (Louisiana Court)
We file the petition with the appropriate parish court and obtain the Judgment of Possession. We handle all court appearances and correspondence.

### Step 6: Recording and Delivery
We record the Judgment of Possession in the conveyance records of every parish where the decedent owned real estate and deliver certified copies to you.

## What Documents Do You Need?

- Certified death certificate (from Louisiana Vital Records if the deceased died in Louisiana)
- Original will, if one exists
- Deed or tax assessment for any Louisiana real estate
- Names, addresses, and Social Security numbers of all heirs
- Marriage certificate (if surviving spouse is an heir)
- Birth certificates for children heirs

## How Long Does It Take?

Most remote successions are completed in **14–21 business days** from the date we receive all required documents. Complex estates with multiple properties or out-of-state domicile may take longer.
